Beginner's Guide to Guitar

First off by choosing to learn guitar welcome to a lifelong challenge. Even though it will test your patience, it is on of the most rewarding and enjoyable challenges that you will ever love. In order to aid you in your journey we have compiled a list of tips that any player beginner or pro will find helpful.

It's a Slow Process

Playing guitar can become extremely frustrating especially when it feels like you aren't moving forward and making progress. Just remember that this can be a lifelong challenge and skill doesn't appear over night. If you feel like you aren't improving try extending your practice time or increasing the difficulty of your practice routine. Pushing yourself and your abilities is the best way to improve. Given enough time and patience you'll be shredding in no time.

Find Your Sound

This is a task that could take some searching and experimentation. Will you be the acoustic player mastering a wide variety of chords and techniques, a blues player who's guitar screams and cries in beautiful arrangements, or will you be the hard rocker head banging to a sweet groove with an amazing solo. The truth is all of these are great directions to focus on. However, my ambitions may be completely different from yours. This is why you should branch out and discover how you want to play. Any path can lead to technical mastery, it's all about what you love.

Practice Makes Perfect

Do you wanna know what separates the greatest guitar players like Jimi Hendrix, Eric Clapton, and John Petrucci from players like you and I? Practice. To put it bluntly practice determines how good you are and how good you can become. Learning to play guitar, like many things in life, is a marathon not a sprint. Take time to invest in yourself. For a beginner I recommend starting out light, pick a few riffs that are easy and that you enjoy playing.

Some great songs to start out on are Smoke on The Water by Deep Purple, or Iron Man by Black Sabbath. If you are leaning toward the acoustic side of things try out Wonder wall by Oasis or Sweet Home Alabama. Both of these songs will help you learn chords which will transform the way you play. Next work out a practice routine that works for you. Starting out I recommend 1 hour a day 5 days a week. This will give you a good idea as to how you will like learning to play.

Choose Your Weapon

This is a lifetime struggle for any musician. "That great guitar sitting in guitar center will make me sound great I know it." I've heard this way to many times and there is truth to it but not ad much as you think. The truth is while having a great guitar will give you an amazing tone, it won't matter if you don't have the skill to play it. But lets say you've used your current guitar to its fullest extent or you are about to buy your first one.

Your price range will determine what guitars are available to you. For beginner electric players, some of the best deals for the money are Squires. Ease of play, afford-ability, and reliability have made Squire a household name in the guitar world. Likewise you could also opt for an Epiphone. Epiphone makes great, affordable versions of Gibson's greatest designs. Epiphone would also be the way to go for an acoustic. I dare say that Epiphone is more well know for its quality and affordable acoustics then they are electrics. To help decide listen to each guitars tone and determine if it matches the style of music you want to play.

Keep an Open Mind and Have fun!

Don't try to become better than someone else, especially your friends. Your biggest competition should be yourself. Plus if you're learning and improving then one day you will become "good". But until that day respect your fellow musicians and if you can learn from them. They have or are facing the same challenges that you do, and that can spark friendships between guitarist. And from those friendships you will learn so much more than you can looking at tabs on your phone.

I hope you enjoyed this list and found some of its content helpful and insightful. Remember to keep checking yourguitarinfo.com for all of your strumming, picking and tapping needs but until next time keep practicing and shred on!!
